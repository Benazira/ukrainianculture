Maslinetsa, also called Butter or Cheesefare Week, is an ancient Slavic holiday marking the transition from winter to spring and symbolizing renewal. Originating in pre-Christian times, it celebrates nature's awakening and honors the sun, reflected in the golden, round pancakes that resemble the sun's shape and warmth. Pancakes, made with various traditional ingredients, are central to the festivities, and each household often has its own unique recipes. Rooted in pagan traditions honoring Veles, the god of farming and cattle, the holiday evolved into a Christian feast preceding Orthodox Lent. Celebrations last a week and include feasting, singing, dancing, games, and fairs. The climax occurs on Forgiveness Sunday, when a straw effigy representing winter is burned to welcome spring, accompanied by a tradition of seeking and offering forgiveness to others.
