History of Ukraine

Історія України

Ukraine's history is marked by periods of independence and foreign domination. In the 14th to 18th centuries, parts of Ukraine were under Lithuanian, Polish, and Russian control. The Cossacks established a self-governing territory known as the Hetmanate. In the 18th century, most of Ukraine fell under Russian rule. In the early 20th century, Ukraine experienced a brief period of independence from 1918 to 1920. However, portions of western Ukraine were ruled by Poland, Romania, and Czechoslovakia during the interwar period. Ukraine then became part of the Soviet Union as the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic. Following the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991, Ukraine declared independence. In 2004, the Orange Revolution led to the election of Viktor Yushchenko as president. In 2014, the Euromaidan protests resulted in the removal of President Yanukovych and the annexation of Crimea by Russia. The conflict in eastern Ukraine escalated into a war between Ukrainian forces and Russian-backed separatists. In 2022, Russia launched a full-scale invasion of Ukraine, leading to ongoing conflict. Despite these challenges, Ukraine has continued to seek closer ties with the United States, European Union, and NATO.
